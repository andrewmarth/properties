Memo: We have found a few things happening more and more at Foreclosure auctions that we want you to be aware of and to be prepared for. Banks and Trustees have the right by law to cancel the foreclosure sale in the State of AZ only if the homeowner files BK before the sale. Therefore- if we buy today and begin the property rehab tomorrow before we receive the deed, we can and may be responsible for the rehab costs if the trustee rescinds the sale and returns our funds.

It can be costly to recoup the rehab expenses. The best way to avoid this, is to wait until deed is in your possession before beginning the property rehab. Another issue to be aware of:  the title company does not always find IRS liens- even when the property is in first lien possession. So, if we purchase a property that has an IRS lien, there could be a 120 days seasoning period in effect until the subject property can be placed on the open market. Just a few things to consider, Thanks!
---
Back-due Property Tax and HOA Fees: All properties are taxed by the county and some properties are located within home owner's associations. Although our software makes a "call" to the county treasurer, there are times when the data is not up to date. If you have a property in mind, it is best to go to http://treasurer.maricopa.gov/ to confirm the amount of property owed (if any). The purchaser becomes responsible for any unpaid property tax. Likewise, the purchaser may be responsible, for a portion of existing homeowners association fees. Also, it is typical that homeowners associations will require a transfer fee from the new owner (you).
---
Drive Reports: We are happy to drive properties for you but please be aware that a drive report does not replace a property inspection. Drives are performed to assess whether properties have exterior flaws that one may discern from the exterior of the property. It may be possible to see if homes are vacant or occupied or if key structures are damaged or missing. For best results, you would be advised to personally drive the properties to verify what is important in your buying decision. Since time is of the essence, our drivers are verifying that the structures are not obviously damaged; we cannot guarantee the condition of the home beyond what one may reasonably perceive with a quick visual scan of the property.
---
HOA Notices & City Notices in the mail: At times HOA and the City Notices are sent to our office as opposed to yours. These notices sometimes do not succinctly make reference to the property. If there is a bill that is due or a notice, you might not receive it in a timely fashion if it is sent to our office. If you are flipping (and especially if you are holding) the property, you will want to contact both the HOA and the City corresponding to your investment property to let them know your mailing address.
